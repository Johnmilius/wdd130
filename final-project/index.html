Who Am I?

From an early age, my life has been guided by a profound curiosity and a passion for continuous learning and growth. The guitar, in particular, has been a significant part of this journey. Over the past few years, it has profoundly impacted my mental health, bringing peace and strengthening my connections with friends and family. Performing and sharing the joy of music through gatherings has allowed me to highlight the light and beauty that the guitar brings. With eight years of playing experience, I have chronicled my journey from the beginning, demonstrating the transformative power of this instrument. Now, as a 21-year-old college student pursuing a bachelor's degree in Computer Science, I remain eager to help others discover the guitar's wonders and how it can enrich their lives.

My Guitar Learning Journey

A Rocky Start: Part 1

My journey to learning the guitar began in a rather unconventional way. When I was in the 7th grade, I thought it would be incredibly cool to try my hand at the guitar. My mom, always supportive of my whims, enrolled me in group guitar lessons at a nearby music store. However, the content didn't resonate with me. We were learning songs like "Mary Had a Little Lamb" and reading from sheet music, which utterly failed to capture my interest and excitement. In hindsight, this wasn't the ideal start for my musical adventure, but it was a beginning nonetheless.

An Unexpected Mentor: Part 2

I only attended the music store classes for a few months before my enthusiasm waned, leaving me feeling discouraged and unmotivated to try again. However, an unexpected twist came later that year. My mom received a call from a close friend who had recently been diagnosed with cancer. In an effort to distract himself from his medical condition, he graciously offered to teach me guitar in a one-on-one setting. This marked a significant turning point in my journey.

Finding My Groove: Part 3

Over the next three years, I took lessons from him in a much more engaging and supportive environment. His personalized teaching methods and the material we covered resonated deeply with me, sparking a genuine passion for the instrument. These lessons were not just about learning to play; they were about connecting with the music on a deeper level and discovering the joy of creating melodies.

Striking a Chord on My Own: Part 4

After those three transformative years, I continued to teach myself, diving into the vast world of guitar on my own. I discovered new skills and techniques, experimenting with different styles and genres. Along the way, I found effective methods to self-learn new songs and developed valuable insights that have not only enhanced my playing but also allowed me to help others on their musical journeys.
