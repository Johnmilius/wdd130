My Guitar Learning Journey

Part 1

My journey to learning the guitar began in an unconventional way. In the 7th grade, I thought it would be cool to try out the guitar, so my mom enrolled me in group guitar lessons at a nearby music store. Unfortunately, the content didn't resonate with me. We were learning songs like "Mary Had a Little Lamb" and reading from sheet music, which failed to capture my interest. In hindsight, this wasn't the ideal start for me.

Part 2

I only attended the music store classes for a few months before losing interest. I felt discouraged and wasn't eager to try again. However, my journey took an unexpected turn later that year when my mom received a call from a close friend who had been diagnosed with cancer. In an effort to distract himself from his medical condition, he offered to teach me guitar in a one-on-one setting.

Part 3

For the next three years, I took lessons from him in a much better environment. His personalized teaching methods and the material we covered made sense to me and kept me engaged.

Part 4

After those three years, I continued to teach myself, discovering new skills and techniques. I've found effective ways to self-learn new songs and have gained valuable insights into helping others on their musical journeys.
